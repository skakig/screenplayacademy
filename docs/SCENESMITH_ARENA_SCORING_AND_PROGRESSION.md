# SceneSmith Arena Scoring & Progression

## Status

Canonical scoring authority and launch model for SceneSmith Arena.

This document supplements and, where scoring authority conflicts, supersedes the judging assumptions in:

- `docs/SCENESMITH_ARENA_INTELLIGENCE_AND_WRITER_DEVELOPMENT.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_INTELLIGENCE_PLATFORM_VISION.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_ACADEMY.md`
- `docs/CHARACTER_TRUTH_ENGINE.md`
- `docs/CHARACTER_TRUTH_ENGINE_SOURCE_SYSTEMS.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_WORLD_BUILDING.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_REVIEW_INTELLIGENCE_AND_ARGUMENT_STUDIO.md`

Arena must work for one user on the first day.

It must not depend on:

- available human judges
- an established user base
- peer voting volume
- followers
- popularity
- creator reputation
- an active host
- staff moderation of every round

The primary launch result is the **SceneSmith Studio Score**.

Fan Votes are a separate social signal that may be added later.

# 1. Executive doctrine

Arena is a gamified application of SceneSmith's full intelligence architecture.

The primary evaluation path is:

```text
Round objective
+ submitted writing
+ project canon when applicable
+ TMH
+ Anti-Thesis
+ Character Truth
+ Relationship Intelligence
+ World / Location Intelligence
+ Story and Continuity Intelligence
+ mode-specific craft rubric
→ SceneSmith Studio evaluation
→ immediate dimensional score
→ evidence-backed result
→ ITS learning update
→ PfHU-adapted explanation
→ next round
→ cumulative writer-development model
```

The AI is not evaluated outside SceneSmith and then decorated with TMH, ITS, or PfHU afterward.

The evaluation runs **through the SceneSmith systems**.

Each system contributes a distinct interpretive frame:

- TMH models moral orientation, responsibility, sacrifice, integrity, regression, aspiration, and behavioral scope.
- The Anti-Thesis models corruption, inversion, collapse, self-deception, domination, exclusion, exploitation, and regressive trajectories.
- Character Truth evaluates whether behavior, voice, knowledge, motivation, relationships, and pressure responses fit the intended character.
- Relationship Intelligence evaluates power, trust, dependency, affection, grievance, loyalty, fear, intimacy, and relational change.
- World and Location Intelligence evaluate lore, geography, culture, institutions, systems, rules, artifacts, travel, and temporal state.
- Story Intelligence evaluates scene function, causality, escalation, reversal, consequence, setup, payoff, pacing, and narrative promise.
- ITS determines what the round demonstrates, what remains unmastered, and what challenge should come next.
- PfHU determines how the result should be presented so this writer can understand and use it.

The system is not claiming value neutrality.

It provides a declared, versioned, inspectable SceneSmith evaluation paradigm.

# 2. SceneSmith Studio Score versus Fan Votes

Arena must maintain separate result channels.

## 2.1 SceneSmith Studio Score

The primary score at launch.

Characteristics:

- available with one user
- immediate
- rubric-based
- multi-dimensional
- evidence-backed
- challenge-specific
- versioned
- repeatable within stated model tolerance
- independent of popularity
- independent of follower count
- independent of subscription tier
- suitable for cumulative ITS learning evidence

Label it clearly:

```text
SceneSmith Studio Score
```

Do not label it:

- objective truth
- universal writing score
- human consensus
- moral worth
- intelligence score
- permanent talent score

## 2.2 Fan Votes

A separate optional social result introduced when sufficient participation exists.

Possible fan reactions:

- favorite overall
- strongest line
- funniest
- most emotional
- most surprising
- most compelling character
- would read/watch more

Label it clearly:

```text
Fan Vote
Audience Favorite
Community Choice
```

Fan Votes do not modify the SceneSmith Studio Score.

## 2.3 Host or expert results

Optional later channels:

```text
Host Selection
Expert Panel
Classroom Instructor
Writers’ Room Decision
```

These remain separate from the SceneSmith score.

## 2.4 Result display

A mature result page may show:

```text
SceneSmith Studio Score     91
Fan Vote                    76% audience preference
Host Selection              Finalist
Personal Improvement        +8 from comparable prior round
```

Never collapse these into one unexplained number.

# 3. TMH and Anti-Thesis are descriptive behavioral rubrics

TMH levels are not writing scores.

```text
Character classified at Level 1
≠
Writing score of 1
```

Correct interpretation:

```text
Intended behavioral paradigm: Level 1 survival / Anti-Thesis pattern
Execution fidelity: 94%
```

A destructive character may receive an excellent SceneSmith Studio Score when the writing expresses that destructive paradigm coherently, dramatically, and consistently.

A supposedly transcendent character may score poorly when the writing merely labels them virtuous without demonstrating the behavior, sacrifice, integrity, relational consequence, or earned transformation.

## 3.1 What TMH evaluates in story

- moral and behavioral center of gravity
- scope of responsibility
- protected values
- sacrificed values
- response to pressure
- regression
- aspiration
- integrity between stated values and action
- treatment of people inside and outside the moral circle
- cost accepted or displaced
- relational and social consequences

## 3.2 What the Anti-Thesis evaluates

- self-deception
- moral inversion
- manipulation
- domination
- exploitation
- tribal exclusion
- grievance identity
- cowardice disguised as prudence
- cruelty disguised as justice
- vanity disguised as virtue
- corruption under power
- regression under fear
- destruction of relationship, institution, or self

## 3.3 Evaluation question

The question is not:

> Is this character good?

The question is:

> How accurately, coherently, and dramatically did the writer express the intended moral-behavioral paradigm under the conditions of this scene?

```text

Start Next Round
```

The score is locked before presentation adapts.

# 7. Evaluation sequence

The canonical launch sequence is:

```text
1. Receive entry.
2. Validate format, ownership, permissions, and challenge constraints.
3. Freeze the round rubric version.
4. Build a bounded context package.
5. Run Story and craft evaluation.
6. Run Character Truth and Relationship evaluation when applicable.
7. Run TMH / Anti-Thesis evaluation when applicable.
8. Run World, Location, lore, timeline, magic, technology, and consequence evaluation when applicable.
9. Run contradiction and alternative-interpretation pass.
10. Calculate dimensional scores and weighted SceneSmith Studio Score.
11. Lock the round result.
12. Show immediate result.
13. Restore writer identity.
14. Apply ITS learning evidence when consent permits.
15. Use PfHU to adapt explanation.
16. Select or offer the next challenge.
17. Update cumulative metrics from comparable evidence.
```

The writer profile must not predetermine the current score.

It may determine:

- recommended challenge
- difficulty
- feedback presentation
- practice sequence

It may not alter:

- rubric weights
- evidence interpretation to favor or punish the writer
- competitive score
- rank

# 8. Evidence and confidence

Every scored dimension must store:

```text
score
confidence
entry evidence
canon evidence when used
counterevidence
missing context
rubric version
model / evaluator version
calculation version
```

## 8.1 Low evidence

When evidence is insufficient:

```text
Not enough evidence to score this dimension reliably.
```

Do not manufacture precision.

## 8.2 Inference versus canon

Label findings as:

```text
EXPLICIT
STRONGLY_INFERRED
PLAUSIBLE_INTERPRETATION
LOW_CONFIDENCE
NOT_APPLICABLE
```

## 8.3 Alternative interpretation

Before finalizing a severe finding, ask:

- Is there a reasonable alternate reading?
- Could this be intentional contradiction?
- Is the narrator unreliable?
- Is the challenge asking for transformation rather than continuity?
- Is required canon missing?

The result may still score the entry poorly, but it must explain uncertainty honestly.

# 9. Round score, cumulative score, and mastery

These are distinct.

## 9.1 Round score

Measures performance on one entry against one rubric.

```text
SceneSmith Studio Score: 91
```

## 9.2 Dimension history

Tracks comparable dimensions across rounds.

```text
Behavioral fidelity
Round 1: 72
Round 2: 81
Round 3: 89
Round 4: 91
```

## 9.3 Series score

A bounded result for a declared sequence of rounds.

Example:

```text
Villain Under Pressure Series
Cumulative SceneSmith Score: 87
```

It should weight:

- comparable round scores
- challenge difficulty
- context diversity
- consistency
- revision improvement
- final transfer task

It must not be a simple arithmetic average when rounds test different capabilities.

## 9.4 Writer mastery state

ITS derives a learning state from repeated evidence.

Possible states:

```text
INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE
EMERGING
DEMONSTRATED
CONSISTENT
STRONG
TRANSFER_DEMONSTRATED
NEEDS_REVIEW
```

## 9.5 No universal permanent score

Do not create:

```text
Writer Rating: 742
Overall Human Worth: 86
Permanent Talent Score: 73
```

A profile may show a dashboard of demonstrated capabilities, trends, and confidence.

## 9.6 Cumulative capability example

```text
Character voice consistency       88
Moral-behavioral fidelity         93
Pressure response                 84
Relational differentiation       79
Subtext                           81
Consequence awareness             76
World integration                 90
Revision responsiveness           87
```

Each value must link to supporting rounds and rubric versions.

# 10. ITS progression engine

ITS uses cumulative evidence to choose the next challenge.

## 10.1 First round

Test a foundational capability.

Example:

> Write the villain’s response to betrayal.

## 10.2 Second round

Change the pressure while preserving the character paradigm.

> The same character now needs help from someone they despise.

## 10.3 Third round

Test moral contradiction or choice.

> The character has a genuine opportunity to show mercy at personal cost.

## 10.4 Revision round

Apply one specific finding.

> Revise the mercy scene so the choice has a visible personal cost without changing the outcome.

## 10.5 Transfer round

Test whether the writer can reproduce the capability elsewhere.

> Write a different character whose corruption is driven by cowardice rather than domination.

## 10.6 Progression principles

- one high-value focus at a time
- immediate result before deep lesson
- increasing difficulty only when evidence supports it
- repeat a capability across different contexts
- distinguish memorized correction from transferable mastery
- return writers to real project work

# 11. PfHU result presentation

PfHU adapts delivery after the score is locked.

It may adapt:

- terminology
- feedback length
- examples
- ordering
- visual presentation
- directness
- number of corrections
- whether to explain TMH terminology
- whether to present a revision or a fresh challenge

It may not alter:

- score
- rank
- rubric
- evidence
- confidence

## 11.1 Example beginner result

> Your villain stayed focused on control even when afraid. That made the character feel consistent. In the next round, show what the villain is willing to lose to keep that control.

## 11.2 Example advanced result

> Anti-Thesis fidelity is high in domination and instrumentalization, but consequence integrity is weaker: the scene grants coercive success without relational, institutional, or strategic cost. Round 2 should test dependence on a despised subordinate.

Same score. Different explanation.

# 14. Fan-vote integrity

Fan voting is useful but vulnerable to manipulation.

Threats include:

- bots
- multiple accounts
- purchased engagement
- follower-count advantage
- coordinated voting
- reciprocal vote rings
- brigading
- creator fame
- unequal audience size

## 14.1 Separation rule

Fan Votes never alter the SceneSmith Studio Score.

## 14.2 Eligibility options

Future fan voting may require:

- authenticated account
- verified email
- minimum account age
- round participation
- project or community membership
- rate limits
- suspicious-vote review

## 14.3 Ranked fairness

For ranked Arena:

- submissions may remain blind until results
- entry order should rotate
- vote counts may remain hidden until voting closes
- self-voting is prohibited
- duplicate and suspicious votes are flagged
- follower counts are not displayed during voting

## 14.4 Small-audience handling

Do not display a statistically misleading percentage from one or two votes as meaningful consensus.

Possible states:

```text
Not enough fan votes
Early audience signal
Community result
```

# 16. Launch sequence correction

The public Arena launch sequence should be:

## Phase A — stabilize existing Arena

- completed Results reachable
- identities correct
- blind self-entry correct
- errors and retries
- mobile
- canonical-script safety

## Phase B — solo SceneSmith scoring

- one or two calibrated modes
- immediate SceneSmith Studio Score
- evidence and confidence
- Round 2 / Round 3 progression
- basic cumulative dimensions
- no human judge dependency
- no fan-vote dependency

## Phase C — Editor and Academy bridge

- apply same lenses to project scenes
- recommend Arena practice from Editor findings
- transfer Arena evidence to private ITS state with consent

## Phase D — multiplayer SceneSmith rounds

- each entry receives SceneSmith Studio Score
- optional comparison and awards
- results independent of fan voting

## Phase E — Fan Votes

- separate audience scoreboard
- anti-manipulation controls
- blind voting where appropriate
- minimum evidence thresholds

## Phase F — expert, classroom, and showcase channels

- host selections
- instructors
- panels
- public events

Human judging is an optional expansion, not an Arena launch requirement.

# 17. Acceptance tests

## 17.1 Zero-user-base test

One new user opens Arena with no other participants.

Expected:

- can start a solo round
- can submit
- receives SceneSmith Studio Score
- receives strongest dimension
- receives one improvement
- can begin Round 2
- no empty judge state
- no request to recruit voters

## 17.2 Villain fidelity test

Prompt asks for a Level 1 or Anti-Thesis-aligned villain under threat.

Expected:

- character is not penalized for being evil
- classification remains separate from craft score
- behavioral fidelity is scored
- self-justification, pressure response, and consequence are evaluated

## 17.3 Virtue-label test

Entry labels a character noble but shows no cost, integrity, or sacrifice.

Expected:

- no automatic high score for claimed virtue
- evidence-based TMH fidelity finding
- missing demonstration explained

## 17.4 Immediate-result test

After evaluation completes:

Expected first view:

- Studio Score
- strongest dimension
- one key improvement
- next-round action

Full explanation remains optional.

## 17.5 Longitudinal test

Complete three related rounds.

Expected:

- independent round scores
- comparable dimension trend
- cumulative series result
- ITS next-round recommendation
- no simple average when skills differ

## 17.6 Profile isolation test

Two writers submit identical entries under the same rubric and context.

Expected:

- same evaluation treatment within model tolerance
- prior profile does not alter score
- PfHU presentation may differ after score lock

## 17.7 Fan-vote separation test

Entry receives low fan preference but high Studio Score.

Expected:

- both displayed separately
- neither overwrites the other
- development profile uses the Studio evaluation and labels fan response separately

## 17.8 Subscription fairness test

Free and Studio users submit identical entries to the same available rubric.

Expected:

- tier does not influence scoring
- differences in context depth are disclosed when applicable

## 17.9 Editor transfer test

A writer improves a weakness through Arena and revises a real scene.

Expected:

- Editor detects improvement using the same dimension definitions
- evidence links remain separate
- mastery update reflects transfer, not merely Arena repetition

## 17.10 Failure test

Evaluation provider fails.

Expected:

- entry preserved
- retry available
- no fabricated score
- no duplicate charge
- next-round progression waits for a valid result

# 18. Final doctrine

```text
Arena launches with SceneSmith Studio judging.
Human judges are optional.
Fan Votes are separate.
TMH and the Anti-Thesis evaluate behavioral fidelity, not moral worth.
The AI runs through SceneSmith’s intelligence systems.
Each round returns an immediate, gratifying result.
ITS turns repeated rounds into cumulative learning evidence.
PfHU adapts explanation after the score is locked.
The current entry is scored independently of reputation or prior profile.
The cumulative profile measures demonstrated capability across contexts.
Arena practice transfers into Editor Review and Academy.
```

The product succeeds when one writer—with no audience, no judges, and no community—can enter Arena, complete a challenge, receive a meaningful SceneSmith Studio Score, understand one thing to improve, start the next round immediately, and become measurably more capable over time.
