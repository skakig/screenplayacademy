# SceneSmith Epic Fantasy & Universe Platform

## Status

Canonical long-term product doctrine, implementation compass, and acceptance standard for epic fantasy, space opera, shared universes, multi-book sagas, multi-era franchises, and other complex secondary-world storytelling.

This document extends, but does not replace:

- `AGENTS.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_INTELLIGENCE_PLATFORM_VISION.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_STORY_PRODUCTION_PLATFORM.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_WORLD_BUILDING.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_ACADEMY.md`
- `docs/CHARACTER_TRUTH_ENGINE.md`
- `docs/CHARACTER_TRUTH_ENGINE_SOURCE_SYSTEMS.md`
- `docs/ITS_PfHU_Importation.md`
- `docs/SCENESMITH_VOICE_STUDIO.md`

This is not a request to implement the entire platform in one pass.

It exists so SceneSmith can grow into a world-class epic-story companion without losing the product’s center of gravity, authorship protections, local-first writing model, canon discipline, evidence model, or provider neutrality.

---

# 1. Executive thesis

SceneSmith must be able to support a creator who is not merely writing one screenplay, but managing an entire living universe.

The product must scale from:

```text
one village
one protagonist
one conflict
one screenplay
```

to:

```text
multiple planets or realms
multiple civilizations
multiple magic or technology systems
multiple eras
multiple calendars
thousands of named entities
interlocking character, political, religious, military, economic, and metaphysical systems
many books, screenplays, games, episodes, adaptations, and production assets
multiple writers and departments
```

The central promise is:

> SceneSmith remembers the universe deeply enough that the creator can keep expanding it without losing its truth, while remaining humble enough never to turn inference into canon without permission.

The platform should help creators:

- discover the world emerging from the story
- define only what the story currently needs
- preserve approved canon and its history
- understand relationships across time and space
- track what each character knows, believes, misunderstands, hides, or lies about
- test magic, technology, political, logistical, and metaphysical rules
- prevent avoidable continuity mistakes
- manage multiple books, projects, timelines, adaptations, and contributors
- visualize geography, planets, realms, routes, factions, history, and character movement
- produce reliable continuation packages for future works
- translate approved canon into visual, editorial, publishing, and production outputs

SceneSmith must not become:

- a blank lore wiki
- an endless questionnaire
- a map editor disconnected from the story
- a generic note database
- an AI encyclopedia generator
- a franchise simulator that silently invents canon
- a living-author impersonation product
- a provider-specific image or map wrapper
- a system that requires the creator to finish the world before writing the story

---

# 2. Complexity benchmark and ethical boundary

A useful internal benchmark is a **Sanderson-scale epic fantasy project**: a hypothetical corpus with many books, interlocking hard systems, hidden historical connections, multiple cultures, changing political control, deep character arcs, layered cosmology, and long-range setup and payoff.

This benchmark is about **complexity, continuity, and scale**.

It is not permission to:

- imitate Brandon Sanderson’s prose style
- present a living author as an AI mentor
- market SceneSmith as an author clone
- reproduce protected characters, worlds, or unpublished materials
- infer that one author’s process is the only correct epic-fantasy process

SceneSmith should support hard, soft, mythic, surreal, literary, folkloric, comedic, grim, romantic, heroic, spiritual, science-fantasy, and hybrid worldbuilding approaches.

The system must adapt to the creator’s project rather than force every story into one model.

# 5. Core operating model

The full platform should support seven connected modes:

```text
DISCOVER   Notice world elements emerging from active writing.
BUILD      Guide the creator through the next meaningful decision.
EXPLORE    View the universe through maps, timelines, graphs, and bibles.
PROTECT    Detect continuity, chronology, rule, knowledge, and identity conflicts.
CONTINUE   Prepare a new book, episode, adaptation, or contributor.
VISUALIZE  Create reviewable visual interpretations and reference packs.
PRODUCE    Compile approved canon into publishing and production requirements.
```

All seven modes must share:

- one identity model
- one evidence model
- one canon lifecycle
- one temporal model
- one permission model
- one asset-lineage model

They must not become disconnected products.

# 7. Canon model

## 7.1 Canon is explicit

SceneSmith must preserve the distinction between:

```text
raw source statement
extracted candidate
inference
proposal
approved fact
approved relationship
approved state
superseded canon
retcon
unreliable narration
character belief
public belief
faction doctrine
religious doctrine
rumor
lie
prophecy
myth
historical uncertainty
alternate continuity
adaptation difference
production interpretation
```

No generated, imported, inferred, or detected statement becomes objective canon automatically.

## 7.2 Canon dimensions

Every canon-bearing record should support:

- universe
- continuity branch
- work scope
- valid story time
- recording time
- source authority
- certainty
- visibility
- spoiler level
- approver
- supersession history

## 7.3 Bitemporal requirement

The system should eventually preserve both:

```text
valid time   When the fact is true inside the story world.
system time  When SceneSmith learned, approved, changed, or superseded it.
```

Example:

```text
The city fell in Year 412.
The author established that fact while drafting Book 3.
The fact was later retconned during Book 5 planning.
```

The database must retain all three moments.

## 7.4 Source authority

Authority is configurable and must not be inferred from upload order.

Example hierarchy:

```text
author-approved master bible
> published canonical edition
> locked manuscript
> approved continuation decision
> current draft
> outline
> research note
> brainstorm
> generated suggestion
```

An adaptation may define a different hierarchy.

## 7.5 Canon locks

Authorized roles may lock:

- entity identity
- spelling
- genealogy
- magic rule
- chronology
- visual canon
- pronunciation
- map geometry
- production interpretation

A lock prevents silent mutation but must not prevent a reviewable change proposal.

# 8. Truth, belief, knowledge, secrecy, and misinformation

Epic stories require more than one truth layer.

SceneSmith must distinguish:

```text

historian interpretation
public belief

faction doctrine
religious doctrine
family secret
classified knowledge
character knowledge
character assumption
character false belief
character lie
character memory
character forgotten knowledge
reader knowledge
writer-only truth
```

## 8.1 Knowledge ledger

Each character may have time-bound knowledge records:

- fact or secret
- learned from
- learned when
- confidence
- interpretation
- whether remembered
- whether believed
- whether disclosed
- whether the knowledge is correct

## 8.2 Spoiler-aware presentation

SceneSmith must support:

- creator view
- collaborator view
- new-writer view
- actor view
- reader-facing companion view
- adaptation team view

A contributor should see only what their role and spoiler clearance allow.

## 8.3 Knowledge continuity examples

SceneSmith may flag:

- a character uses a post-war place name before learning it
- a character recognizes a symbol they have never encountered
- a character knows a secret revealed only in another plotline
- a dead character appears to pass information after death without an established mechanism
- a character remembers an event erased by time manipulation

Every finding must cite evidence and allow intentional exceptions.

- cultural conflict
- species conflict

## 29.2 Severity

```text
INFO        Interesting discrepancy or unanswered question.
NOTICE      Possible inconsistency worth review.
IMPORTANT   Likely reader-visible contradiction.
CRITICAL    Breaks core canon, chronology, identity, or causality.
```

Severity must not be used to block writing.

## 29.3 Finding anatomy

- affected source
- established canon
- conflicting text
- confidence

- likely options
- dismiss
- mark intentional
- create proposal
- resolve

## 29.4 No uncited certainty

The system must never say “This contradicts canon” without showing the canon and source.

# 35. Performance and scale requirements

A professional universe may contain:

- millions of words
- thousands of entities
- tens of thousands of facts
- many source documents
- many visual assets
- many timeline events
- many contributors

## 35.1 Loading strategy

- lazy-load graph neighborhoods
- paginate evidence and appearances
- cache summaries
- index aliases and full text
- precompute common continuation views
- avoid loading the entire universe into one client state
- use background jobs for extraction and continuity scans

## 35.2 Writer’s Desk isolation

The writing path must remain local-first.

World extraction, graph updates, and continuity analysis must occur outside the keystroke/render path.

```text
User input → local document → rendered writing surface → background sync → asynchronous discovery
```

Never:

```text
User input → world extraction → database mutation → rendered writing surface
```

## 35.3 Large map performance

- viewport-based feature loading
- level-of-detail rendering
- clustering
- layer visibility control
- simplified geometry at low zoom
- deferred image loading

## 35.4 Search performance

Search should use:

- canonical-name index
- alias index
- full-text index
- relationship index
- time-range index
- universe and continuity scope
- permission filters

# 42. Required difficult-data wireframes

Before implementation, create wireframes using realistic difficult cases.

At minimum:

1. A capital city renamed three times across four eras.
2. Two unrelated characters with the same name.
3. One character using a title, alias, and secret identity.
4. A prophecy that is publicly misunderstood.
5. Two calendars with uncertain conversion.
6. A disputed border with de jure and de facto control.
7. A portal that works only during one season.
8. An artifact owned by three people in overlapping accounts.
9. A retcon that affects five books and one adaptation.
10. A family tree with adoption, disputed parentage, and a hidden heir.
11. A magic exception that appears to break a foundational rule.
12. A character who knows a secret in one continuity but not another.
13. A city destroyed in the novel but preserved in the film adaptation.
14. A non-geographic conceptual realm.
15. A star route with time dilation.

Do not validate UX using only clean demo data.

# 43. Acceptance tests

## 43.1 Writing remains primary

Write continuously for thirty seconds while background discovery runs.

Expected:

- no focus loss
- no caret jump
- no blocked typing
- no first-character loss
- no automatic canon insert

## 43.2 New location discovery

Mention an unknown place in a chapter.

Expected:

- quiet candidate
- source evidence
- possible entity type
- no blocking modal

## 43.3 Identity resolution

Import sources containing:

```text
THE EMPEROR
EMPEROR KAEL
KAEL IV
THE ASH KING
```

Expected:

- possible-identity proposal
- evidence for each label
- no automatic merge
- human-confirmed resolution
- aliases preserved

## 43.4 Same-name distinction

Two different people are both named Tomas.

Expected:

- no destructive merge
- co-occurrence and context shown
- separate canonical entities possible

## 43.5 Temporal location state

A city changes rulers and names.

Expected:

- one or more entities according to human decision
- time-bound names and control
- era scrubber updates
- old references remain valid in prior eras

## 43.6 Character knowledge

A character speaks a secret before learning it.

Expected:

- knowledge continuity finding
- established learning event cited
- conflicting scene cited
- intentional override possible

## 43.7 Travel

A journey crosses mountains in winter faster than established rules allow.

Expected:

- plausible time range
- assumptions shown
- route evidence
- options presented
- no automatic rewrite

## 43.8 Magic rule

A spell bypasses a previously universal cost.

Expected:

- rule and exception cited
- severity based on narrative impact
- classify as error, new exception, unreliable report, or intentional change

## 43.9 Calendar conversion

Two cultures record the same event differently.

Expected:

- both dates preserved
- conversion confidence shown
- no silent normalization

## 43.10 Political control

A map displays a contested city.

Expected:

- de jure claim
- de facto control
- uncertainty or dispute visualized
- era dependence

## 43.11 Artifact custody

An artifact appears in two simultaneous scenes.

Expected:

- custody conflict
- both sources cited
- clone, copy, timeline issue, or error options

## 43.12 Retcon

Approve a retcon affecting multiple works.

Expected:

- impact report
- old canon preserved
- affected records proposed for migration
- no silent mass overwrite

## 43.13 Adaptation branch

Change an event only in the film adaptation.

Expected:

- novel continuity unchanged
- adaptation branch updated
- shared entities remain linked

## 43.14 Spoiler access

A new collaborator has Book One clearance only.

Expected:

- later-book secrets hidden
- permitted evidence visible
- no spoiler leakage through search or AI answers

## 43.15 Map movement

Move a city on the Atlas.

Expected:

- impact preview
- travel and border effects
- proposal rather than immediate destructive change when canon is affected

## 43.16 Visual generation

Expected:

- editable brief
- canon snapshot
- provider/model/settings/cost
- candidate status
- human approval required

## 43.17 RLS

Test owner, co-writer, continuity editor, map editor, commenter, viewer, and non-member.

Expected:

- role-correct access
- no cross-project leakage
- no spoiler leakage

# 46. Canonical terminology

Use:

- **Epic Fantasy & Universe Platform** — the complete complex-world capability defined here
- **Universe** — highest shared fictional or historical world container
- **Continuity Branch** — controlled canon branch
- **World Bible** — human-readable approved reference
- **World Graph** — structured, temporal, evidence-backed universe model
- **World Entity** — any canonical person-adjacent world object, place, group, system, event, or concept
- **World Fact** — a time-bound claim about an entity
- **World State** — facts valid at a selected story time
- **Truth Layer** — objective truth, belief, doctrine, rumor, lie, or related knowledge category
- **World Proposal** — unapproved detected, imported, inferred, generated, or edited item
- **World Review Inbox** — proposal and contradiction review workspace
- **World Atlas** — spatial and network visualization over the World Graph
- **Continuation Package** — evidence-backed state for the next work or contributor
- **Visual Canon** — human-approved visual reference
- **Retcon Proposal** — reviewable change to prior approved canon
- **Knowledge Ledger** — time-aware record of what a character knows or believes

# 47. Final doctrine

SceneSmith’s epic-fantasy capability must obey these principles:

```text
The Writer’s Desk remains primary.
Let the story create the need for worldbuilding.
Scale from one room to a multi-era universe.
Separate universe, series, work, version, adaptation, and continuity.
Keep identity human-controlled.
Extract candidates, not automatic truth.
Keep canon explicit, temporal, and evidence-backed.
Preserve objective truth, belief, rumor, secrecy, and character knowledge separately.
Model geography, politics, culture, systems, artifacts, and history as connected parts of one world.
Treat maps as views over the World Graph, not isolated pictures.
Use ITS to determine what matters next.
Use PfHU to present it without overwhelming the creator.
Use Character Truth and TMH to understand pressure, not to stereotype or morally grade.
Keep providers replaceable.
Keep generated assets traceable and human-approved.
Preserve authorship, permissions, history, and intellectual property.
Never imitate a living author’s voice or persona.
Never make worldbuilding a prerequisite for writing.
```

The platform succeeds when a creator can write one scene today, expand it into a book tomorrow, connect that book to a ten-volume saga years later, invite collaborators safely, adapt it for screen or interactive media, and still answer—with evidence—what is true, when it is true, who knows it, where it happened, why it matters, and what changed.
